The inventory planning gap

Planning systems can produce recommendations, but the decision process often escapes into spreadsheets, inboxes, and undocumented judgment calls. This MVP turns recommendation review into a governed workflow.

🧩

Fragmented ownership

SKU-location parameters are reviewed across teams with inconsistent context and handoffs.

🔍

Low trust

Planners need to understand why a recommendation exists before changing inventory policy.

📝

Manual overrides

Approvals and rejections often happen without structured reasons or categories.

🕰️

Weak audit trail

Leadership needs to know who decided, what changed, why it changed, and when.

What the MVP does

A focused set of capabilities for governing inventory parameter recommendations from intake to decision.

📦

Parameter Intelligence Hub

Upload and inspect SKU-location parameters including safety stock, lot size, service target, unit cost, data quality status, and risk status.

📥

Recommendation Intake

Upload recommended safety stock and lot-size changes, match them to existing parameters, and flag invalid or unmatched rows.

💡

Explainability

Generate grounded explanations that show what data was available, what changed, and what trade-offs the planner should consider.

🧮

What-if Lite

Run transparent directional calculations for inventory value, working capital, lot-size change, and service risk direction.

⚖️

Governance Workflow

Approve, reject, or escalate recommendations with reason capture, categorization, decision history, and audit events.

🧾

Audit Visibility

View recommendation-level events so decisions are traceable, reviewable, and ready for operational governance.

What-if Lite is a directional decision-support estimate. It is not a full MEIO simulation.

Govern every recommendation from intake to decision

The platform creates a clean decision path: bring in the data, review the recommendation, explain the why, test the impact, capture the decision, and preserve the trail.

Why this is different

MetaFlow does not try to replace enterprise planning systems. It sits above them as a decision governance layer, turning recommendations into explainable, simulated, approved, rejected, escalated, and auditable decisions.

🌉

Vendor-neutral overlay

Works as a governance layer around recommendations without forcing a planning-system replacement.

🎯

Decision-first workflow

Focuses on the human decision path: explain, compare, approve, reject, escalate, and learn.

🛡️

Audit-ready governance

Captures the who, what, why, and when behind inventory parameter decisions.
