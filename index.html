Platform modules

Each module supports a practical planning question: what inventory exists, what customers may need next, what should be ordered, what risks could appear, and what work can be automated.

Module What it does Decision value
Inventory Control Hub Maintains product catalog, SKU records, stock by location, supplier and manufacturer details, purchase orders, sales orders, stock movements, barcode actions, batch tracking, serial tracking, labels, reports, and audit trails. Creates a trusted operational view of what stock exists, where it is, and how it moved.
Inventory visibility
POS Data Layer Ingests sales, returns, store activity, product movement, promotion signals, seasonal patterns, and stockout indicators from connected point-of-sale systems. Turns sales activity into clean demand signals for forecasting and replenishment.
Demand signal
Demand Forecasting Engine Uses statistical time series models and machine learning models to generate SKU, store, warehouse, region, and category-level forecasts with confidence ranges. Helps teams plan ahead instead of reacting after stock problems appear.
Predictive planning
Replenishment Optimizer Applies reinforcement learning-based planning for multi-echelon inventory networks, including suppliers, warehouses, distribution centers, and stores. Recommends what to order, how much to order, when to order, and where to place stock.
MEIO planning
Simulation Studio Tests what-if scenarios for demand changes, supplier delays, lead-time shifts, safety stock adjustments, promotions, warehouse shortages, transfers, and new locations. Makes risk visible before teams commit inventory, capital, or operational effort.
Risk lens
Agentic AI Layer Uses AI agents to monitor exceptions, explain forecasts, recommend replenishment actions, answer natural language questions, generate summaries, and support approvals. Converts planning signals into guided, explainable, and partially automated workflows.
AI assistance
Tenant and Governance Layer Supports tenant-based data isolation, role-based access, configurable workflows, audit logs, API-first integration, secure processing, and scalable compute jobs. Provides the SaaS foundation needed for multiple business customers on one platform.

What users can ask the AI agents

The agentic layer helps planners, buyers, inventory teams, and business leaders ask operational questions in plain English and receive context-aware answers.

⚠️

Inventory Risk Questions

“Which SKUs are at risk of stockout next week?” “Which locations have excess inventory?” “Which items need urgent replenishment?”

🔍

Forecast Explanation

“Why did the forecast increase for this product?” “Which sales patterns changed?” “Was the change caused by promotion, seasonality, or recent demand?”

🚚

Replenishment Guidance

“What should we reorder this week?” “Which warehouses should supply which stores?” “How much inventory should be placed at each node?”

🧭

Scenario Analysis

“What happens if supplier lead time increases?” “What if demand rises by 20%?” “What if we reduce safety stock for slow-moving items?”
